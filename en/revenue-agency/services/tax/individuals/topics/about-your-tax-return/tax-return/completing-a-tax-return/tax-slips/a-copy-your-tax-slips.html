Tax slips: Get a copy of your slips

From Canada Revenue Agency

  • Get a copy of your tax slips
  • Understand your tax slips

What type of tax slip do you want?

  • T4, T5, T3, RC62, RC210 or RRSP slips (CRA)
  • OAS, EI, CCB (Service Canada)
Continue

Your employer, payer, administrator or financial institution should provide you with the tax slip you need. You should get most of your slips and receipts by the end of February. However, T3, and T5013 slips come later, in early April.

How do you want to get a copy of your tax slip?

  • From the issuer of the slip
  • From the Canada Revenue Agency
  • I’ll estimate my income manually

Get tax slips from the issuer

Ask the issuer of the slip (your employer, payer, administrator, or financial institution) for a copy of the slip.

Get tax slips from the CRA

Online:

You may be able to view your tax slips online if you use My Account, and if the issuer has sent them to the CRA.

Sign in to My Account

Before you call

To verify your identify, you'll need

  • Social Insurance Number
  • Full name and date of birth
  • Your complete address
  • An assessed return, notice of assessment or reassessment, other tax document, or be signed in to My Account

If you are calling the CRA on behalf of someone else, you must be an authorized representative.

Estimate your income manually

If you can’t get a slip in time to file your tax return, you can estimate your income manually. Add up your pay stubs or financial statements to estimate the income to report, as well as any related deductions and credits you can claim.

Include a note with your return stating the name and address the issuer of the slip, the type of income, and what you are doing to get the slip.

  • If you file electronically: Keep all of your documents in case the CRA asks to see them later.
  • If you file by paper: Attach a copy of the pay stubs or statements and your note to your paper return and keep the original documents.
